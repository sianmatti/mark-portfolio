Overview

Splash Park Bacolod needed to improve event turnout while maintaining cost efficiency. Campaigns were generating traffic, but performance was inconsistent and difficult to scale.

The issue was not budget — it was how audiences, retargeting, and campaign structure were set up.

The Problem

Event campaigns were running, but results lacked consistency.

  • Audience targeting was too broad and not segmented by intent
  • Retargeting was underutilized and lacked structured windows
  • Campaign structure did not clearly separate cold vs warm traffic
  • There was limited visibility into which segments were actually driving turnout

This led to inefficient spend and missed opportunities to convert interested users.

Diagnosis

Traffic was being generated, but not properly nurtured.

Cold audiences were treated the same as warm audiences, and remarketing was not strong enough to capture high-intent users who had already shown interest.

Without proper segmentation and retargeting depth, the system relied too heavily on constant new traffic.

Strategy

The focus was to build a cleaner acquisition system, not just improve ads.

  • Separate prospecting vs retargeting campaigns
  • Build structured remarketing windows (7D / 14D / 30D)
  • Improve audience signal quality
  • Support decisions with clearer GA4 visibility

Execution

1. Audience Segmentation

Audiences were redefined based on intent: cold audiences, warm audiences, and higher-intent users who had already engaged with the brand.

This made it easier to control budget and match campaign messaging to user readiness.

2. Campaign Restructure

Campaigns were reorganized into prospecting and retargeting lanes with clearer roles, instead of asking one structure to do everything at once.

3. Remarketing System

Instead of relying on a single retargeting pool, structured 7-day, 14-day, and 30-day windows were introduced to improve message relevance and timing.

4. Tracking & Visibility

GA4 support improved visibility into how users moved through the funnel and which audience segments contributed most to turnout.

Results

  • +35% increase in event turnout
  • Improved cost efficiency across campaigns
  • Stronger contribution from retargeting segments
  • More predictable performance during campaign periods

Key Takeaway

The improvement did not come from increasing budget. It came from fixing how the system worked: clearer audience segmentation, better retargeting structure, and stronger alignment between traffic and intent.

Once those were in place, performance became easier to scale and more consistent.
