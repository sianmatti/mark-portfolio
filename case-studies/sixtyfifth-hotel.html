Overview

SixtyFifth Hotel needed to improve direct bookings while reducing inefficiencies in paid traffic.

Campaigns were active, but traffic quality was inconsistent, and cost per click was higher than necessary. The system was generating visibility, but not enough qualified demand.

The Problem

The account was driving traffic, but not the right traffic.

  • Brand and non-brand campaigns were not clearly separated
  • Keyword targeting lacked precision, leading to low-intent clicks
  • Negative keywords were underutilized
  • Campaign structure did not fully align with the booking journey

As a result, budget was being spent on users who were less likely to convert.

Diagnosis

The account was capturing demand, but not controlling it.

High-intent searches were mixed with lower-intent discovery traffic, making optimization harder and reducing efficiency.

Strategy

The focus was to tighten acquisition quality and improve control over traffic.

  • Separate brand vs non-brand campaigns
  • Improve keyword targeting precision
  • Reduce wasted spend through negative keywords
  • Support decisions with clearer tracking and reporting

Execution

1. Campaign Restructure

Campaigns were split into brand and non-brand lanes so budget allocation and reporting could reflect search intent more clearly.

2. Keyword Optimization

Keyword lists were refined to prioritize stronger booking intent, reduce weak search variations, and improve overall traffic quality.

3. Negative Keyword Cleanup

Low-quality and irrelevant traffic was reduced through targeted negative keyword refinement and ongoing search term review.

4. Remarketing Support

Retargeting was used to re-engage users who showed booking intent but did not convert on the first interaction.

5. Tracking & Visibility

GA4 support improved visibility into booking behavior and helped identify where acquisition efforts were contributing most effectively.

Results

  • +20% increase in direct bookings
  • -30% reduction in CPC
  • Improved traffic quality and conversion intent
  • More efficient budget allocation across campaigns

Key Takeaway

The improvement did not come from increasing traffic volume. It came from improving traffic quality and control: clearer separation of intent, stronger keyword targeting, and reduced wasted spend.

Once the system was aligned with how users search and book, performance became more predictable and scalable.
